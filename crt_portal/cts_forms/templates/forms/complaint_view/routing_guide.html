Field guide for report routing

(last updated: July 2022)

This guide is intended to help intake specialists

This is an internal guide designed to help intake specialists know when to redirect violation reports to other sections.

Why is it important to reroute reports correctly and quickly?

We want to give the section best-positioned to analyze any given report the chance to do so as soon as possible.

Step 1.

Does the writer present a threat to themselves or others?

Yes Print a copy of the original incoming report (correspondent information, reported issue, personal description) and email the PDF to Theresa.Weathers@usdoj.gov. Federal Protective Services (FPS) will follow-up with any relevant federal agency and the correspondent.

No further action is necessary by CRT and the report can be closed.
No Continue to next step.

Final Note

This guide is designed to be practical, not precise. The federal civil rights laws we enforce are varied and complex. When we list a section for routing, it does not mean that the section will necessarily have enforcement authority for that report. It also does not mean this section is the only one who might have enforcement authority. Being listed here only means this section is likely best positioned to know the right next step for this kind of report.
